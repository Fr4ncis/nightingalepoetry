Ode To Asparagus

Asparagus, you timely shoot, You portent of the spring, Volley through the golden dew, Pierce your purple blaxing sting; Spear the cloying mud and grass, Burst them with your mast, Break into the waking summer, Brave until the last.

Asparagus, though we steal your youth, Embalm it with our oils, sear your shins and soothe your fresh wounds, Boil you for our dames and hommes; Asparagus, you shall slip your Kismet, From the bowels of purgatory, When your spirit lifts above us, In the perfume of our wee.

And as the cuckoo drops its song, About the flurry of the farms, And as the martin starts to long Towards the pitch of longer climes; Asparagus, you spread your arms, And many arms will spring from these, And we will watch you breaking outwards, Through the matrix of your seed.

Thomas Ironmonger
